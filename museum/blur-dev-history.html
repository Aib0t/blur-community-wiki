Introduction

Blur 1 and 2 had a troubled, obscure and bizarre development. This article is an attempt to preserve any and all information, related to Blur 1 and 2 “making of” story, using every piece of info available. There will be mentions of events, cut features and less known things about Blur 1 and 2 development.

In short - Blur development went thought 3 major stages:

  1. “Mad Max”/Neon PGR stage - pre-pre-production stage of development.
  2. Orange stage - game featured orange UI and main story was still intact.
  3. Blur stage - game featured blue UI and main story was removed.

Blur 1

Late 2007

26 September 2007 - publisher Activision acquired Bizarre Creations. for $107.4m; $67.4m immediately payable with a further $40m contingent hitting certain goals over a 5-year period.

"Activision's independent studio model will continue giving us creative freedom to build exciting new brands. Their financial support and marketing prowess will also enable us to build upon the commercial success that we currently enjoy, and take the company - and our games - to an even higher level."

Early 2008

Late 2007 - Early 2008 - Development of Blur begins.

Finally, we can talk about Blur! It seems like ages since we started on this one, and it's a great relief to finally tell you guys what we're working on! If you're a long time Bizarre community member you're probably used to these periods of relative silence from the studio - we're running dark whilst everybody in the building is fully heads down on the next big thing. We've been working hard on this new racer pretty much as soon as we finished up on PGR4, joining forces with Activision on the way. It's been a long year or so!

Announcing Blur! - Wednesday, May 20 2009

As will be stated after closure “Bizarre's studio atmosphere and creativity were gradually steamrollered once Activision bought it and started calling the shots.”

"We weren't an independent studio making 'our' games anymore - we were making games to fill slots. Although we did believe in them, they were more the products of committees and analysts. The culture we’d worked on for so long gradually eroded just enough so that it wasn’t ‘ours’ anymore."

Former Managing Director Martyn Chudley - April 06, 2011

Mid 2008

Earliest concepts available was created during this time. They show destruction derby style of tracks and almost post-apocalyptic aesthetic. 

Aidan Ward illustrations, who during that time was responsible for “…creating preliminary mood concept art…” 

 

Late 2008

4 December 2008 - First ever mention of Blur happened in the article “Activision signs 18-game ad deal with Massive”

The deal with Activision is primarily focused is on in-game ads however, and covers Xbox 360 and PC titles, including Tony Hawk games, Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen and AMAX Racing.
